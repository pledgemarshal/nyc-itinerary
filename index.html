Thursday — Land easy, end high

Hottest day (~92°F, dry) · Sunset 8:26 PM · Arrival day = two things, not six

  • Arrival

    Check in — The Evelyn

    Room not ready? The desk holds bags. Coffee at Maman around the corner, or Caffè Tusk inside the hotel. Then unpack, shower, do nothing. It's 92° out — the day starts at 5:30.

    7 E 27th St, NoMad
  • 5:30 PM

    First look: Madison Square Park + the Flatiron

    Five minutes from your door. Her first postcard shot is the Flatiron from the park's south end. Split a Shake Shack burger at the original stand if you're peckish — dinner is late tonight.

    5-min walkFreeDirections
  • 6:15 PM

    Head to Grand Central → SUMMIT One Vanderbilt, sunset slot — booked ✓

    Take the 6 train from 28th St two stops (10 min door to door ~25 with buffer). The entrance is inside the Grand Central complex — walk through the main concourse first: the green celestial ceiling, and the Whispering Gallery outside the Oyster Bar (stand in opposite corners of the arch and whisper — she'll hear every word). Then 90 minutes of mirrored sky rooms as the sun drops at 8:26.

    Wear something colorful — the floors are mirrored too (heads-up on her outfit), and heels aren't allowed.

    Subway 6 · ~25 min all-in$44–58 pp45 E 42nd St
  • 9:00 PM

    Dinner — Upland

    Glowing, jar-lined Californian-Italian, two minutes from your bed. Note on the booking that it's her first trip to New York — NYC kitchens genuinely do nice things with that.

    2-min walk from hotel~$70–90 pp345 Park Ave S

Only if you're still standing: a nightcap at The Tusk Bar inside your own hotel (zero effort), or the classic Empire State view at 230 Fifth, two minutes away. Skipping both is also correct — you just landed.

Friday — West Side morning, Village night

86–89°F · Small chance of a brief afternoon storm (you'll be resting) · Sunset 8:26 PM

  • 10:00 AM

    Graze breakfast — Chelsea Market

    Old Nabisco factory turned food hall. The one order that matters: the adobada taco at Los Tacos No. 1. Share things; lunch is a pizza slice.

    Cab 15–20 min (not 8 — it's crosstown)$15–25 pp75 Ninth Ave
  • 11:00 AM

    Walk the High Line, southbound

    Enter at the 16th St elevator next to the market, amble 40 minutes to the Gansevoort St end. Gardens on old rail tracks, Hudson on your right. Free, no ticket. Little Island is 3 minutes from the exit — add it only if you're both feeling it.

    Free16th St entrance
  • 12:15 PM

    West Village stroll → the classic slice at Joe's Pizza

    Wander east through the prettiest streets in Manhattan — Grove St (the Friends corner), Commerce St — to Washington Square's arch and fountain. Lunch is the canonical fold-and-eat slice at Joe's on Carmine. $4, standing up, perfect.

    ~1 hr amble$4–8 pp7 Carmine St
  • 2:00 PM

    Back to the hotel. Rest is the plan, not a gap.

    Cab home, A/C, nap, shower. If a storm pops this afternoon, it happens while you're horizontal — and tonight is all indoors anyway.

    Cab ~15 min
  • 6:15 PM

    Head to the Comedy Cellar — 7:00 PM show

    The most famous comedy club in America: a tiny brick basement on MacDougal where the lineup is a surprise and A-listers drop in unannounced. Doors before the show — be there by 6:40. The reservation is free (book the 7:00 show now; Fridays sell out); you pay $25 each plus a two-item minimum at the table.

    Ninety minutes of laughing in the dark together — the best first-trip date in the Village.

    Cab 15 min or N/R/W to 8th St$25 pp + 2 items117 MacDougal St
  • 9:00 PM

    Dinner — Morandi

    Keith McNally's rustic candlelit Italian — same family as Balthazar, all West Village charm. It's a 10-minute stroll from the Cellar through the prettiest streets in the city, at night. 9:00 and 9:30 tables confirmed open on Resy as of July 10 — L'Artusi (the original plan) is booked solid, so this is the play. Then home; Saturday is the big one.

    10-min walk from the Cellar~$70–100 pp211 Waverly Pl

Rather swoon than laugh? Ms. Lisa Fischer (the voice of 20 Feet from Stardom) plays Blue Note July 16–19 — take the 10:30 PM show and move dinner to 8:00 (Morandi has 8:30 tables too). The Village Vanguard's 10 PM set (Ben Wendel Quintet, ~$40) is the jazz-history version, 3 minutes from dinner.

Saturday — Central Park, then Broadway

Best weather of the trip (86–89°F, low humidity) · The one early start — the rowboat is why

  • 9:00 AM

    Real bagels — Ess-a-Bagel, then the subway uptown

    On the way to Herald Square: giant hand-rolled bagels — one with a schmear, one with lox, split both. Then the N/R/W from 34th St to Fifth Av/59th St, straight to the park's front door.

    12-min walk + 15-min subway$8–14 pp108 W 32nd St
  • 9:45 AM

    Central Park: The Mall → Bethesda Terrace → the rowboats

    Walk the elm cathedral of The Mall to Bethesda Fountain (the movie location), then be at the Loeb Boathouse by 10:30 — rowboats are first-come, card-only, and the line forms by 11. $30 for the boat, an hour on The Lake, Bow Bridge drifting past. You row. She laughs. This is the trip highlight that costs $30.

    Free park · $30/boat/hrBoathouse, E 72nd St
  • 12:15 PM

    Strawberry Fields → the warm cookie at Levain Bakery

    Exit past the Imagine mosaic to the original Levain on W 74th. One warm 6-oz chocolate-chip-walnut cookie, two forks. (You'll regret splitting it. Get two.)

    10-min walk$5–7167 W 74th St
  • 1:15 PM

    Home. Afternoon completely off.

    Subway from 72nd St (~35 min door to door) or a cab. Then nothing until dinner: nap, pool, order room service, whatever. You've done the park; tonight is theater. Two anchors, and the space between them is the luxury.

    Subway ~35 min / cab ~25 min
  • 5:45 PM

    Pre-theater dinner — The Clocktower

    Michelin-starred, gorgeous rooms inside the Met Life clock tower, 4 minutes' walk from the hotel — and, unlike the famous names, actually bookable this week. Tell them you have an 8 PM curtain; they'll pace you out by 7:10.

    4-min walk~$110–150 pp5 Madison Ave
  • 7:15 PM

    Subway to the theater — not a cab

    N/Q/R/W from 28th St to Times Sq–42nd St, then a 5-minute walk up to the Al Hirschfeld. A 7:30 Saturday-night cab into the Theater District is how people miss curtain — Broadway holds for no one.

    Subway + walk ~20 min · $2.90
  • 8:00 PM

    Moulin Rouge! The Musical — Al Hirschfeld Theatre

    The most romantic show on Broadway — Paris, champagne, sequins, and "Come What May" — and it closes August 30, so this is genuinely your only chance to see it together. Verified running July 18 at 8 PM, from ~$110 at face value; buy direct, skip resale. Afterward, walk five minutes into Times Square lit up at night — mandatory once, then escape downtown.

    From ~$110 pp — buy direct302 W 45th St

Want something no one back home has done? Masquerade — an immersive, masked Phantom of the Opera for 60 guests at a time, from the Sleep No More lineage — has 7:00–8:15 PM entries July 18 (~$205 pp, selling out fast). Is she a baseball fan? Yankees–Dodgers at Yankee Stadium, 8:08 PM, from $65 — a marquee series and peak "only in New York." Museum people? Swap the afternoon rest for 90 minutes at The Met — but never the park and the Met and a show in one day.

Sunday — Slow brunch, Brooklyn finale

Coolest day (77–86°F) · Morning rain clears by ~1 PM — the plan is indoors until then · Sunset 8:25 PM

  • 10:30 AM

    Sleep in. Pack. Coffee downstairs.

    Arrange late checkout or leave bags with the desk. If it's raining, it's raining on your coffee cup, not your plans.

  • 11:30 AM

    Brunch — Balthazar, SoHo

    The iconic Parisian brasserie — movie-set room, rain-proof, made for a lazy Sunday. Afterward, an easy stroll through SoHo's cast-iron blocks and Nolita. Still hungry somehow? The pepperoni square at Prince Street Pizza is a 5-minute detour.

    Cab 10–12 min from hotel$40–60 pp80 Spring St
  • 3:30 PM

    Subway to the Brooklyn Bridge → walk it toward Brooklyn

    6 train from Spring St to Brooklyn Bridge–City Hall, then onto the boardwalk. Walking toward Brooklyn puts the skyline behind her for every photo. Give it a full hour with stops; take the first off-ramp toward Washington St.

    Subway 12 min + 60-min walkFreeBridge entrance
  • 5:15 PM

    DUMBO: the Washington St shot + Jane's Carousel

    The Manhattan Bridge framed down Washington St is the photo. Then a $3 spin on the 1922 carousel in its glass box on the river, and the waterfront lawns until dinner.

    On footCarousel $3Washington & Water St
  • 6:30 PM

    Farewell dinner — The River Café

    Michelin star directly under the bridge: flowers, piano, the skyline floating outside the window. The most romantic dining room in New York, full stop. Booking is phone-only — call 718‑522‑5200 (open Wed–Sun, 5:00–10:30 PM). Jacket required, no jeans — this is why you packed one.

    5-min walk$205 pp prix fixe + drinks1 Water St, Brooklyn
  • 8:25 PM

    Sunset over the skyline, then a yellow cab home over the bridge

    Two minutes to the Pebble Beach lawn: the sun drops behind the buildings you just walked from. The cab ride back over the bridge, lights on the water, is the finale itself.

    Cab home ~25 min · $30–40

Flying out Sunday night? Move dinner to 5:30 (they open at 5) and allow 75+ min to JFK/EWR from Brooklyn by car. Want the same view for half the bill? Cecconi's Dumbo is on the same block — terrace, skyline, ~$450 saved.

Everything else you need

Six things to know, one secret weapon

Getting aroundNo MetroCard — tap your phone or credit card at any turnstile ($2.90). Subway wins daytime; cabs win late-night.
HeatThursday hits 92°: water bottle, sunscreen, outdoors before noon and after 5. Every interior is arctic — she'll want a light layer.
ReservationsNYC holds tables 15 minutes. Running late, call. Add "her first New York trip" to every booking note.
Tipping20% at restaurants, $2/drink at bars, 15–20% in cabs.
PackingOne jacket (River Café requires it), broken-in shoes (15k+ steps/day), pocket umbrella Fri–Sun.
The secret weaponPatent Pending — a speakeasy behind a fake coffee counter, 3 min from the hotel. Don't tell her where you're going.
Budget for two, all-in: ≈ $2,200–2,600. Comedy Cellar (~$100 for two, all-in) costs less than the sail did; River Café's prix fixe rose to $205 pp. Biggest lever: swap the River Café for Cecconi's and save ~$450 with the same view.
